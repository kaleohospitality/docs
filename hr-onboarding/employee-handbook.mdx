**This Employee Handbook is not a contract.** It does not create a contract, express or implied, guaranteeing you any specific term of employment. Kaleo Hospitality reserves the right to modify any of our policies and procedures, including those covered in this Handbook, at any time.

## Employment Policies

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="Employment-At-Will">
Your employment with Kaleo Hospitality is a voluntary one and is subject to termination by you or Kaleo Hospitality at will, with or without cause, and with or without notice, at any time. Nothing in these policies shall be interpreted to conflict with or to eliminate or modify in any way the employment-at-will status of Kaleo Hospitality employees.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Equal Employment Opportunity">
Kaleo Hospitality is an equal opportunity employer. We will extend equal opportunity to all individuals without regard to race, religion, color, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), national origin, disability, age, genetic information, or any other status protected under applicable federal, state, or local laws.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Recruitment and Hiring">
Kaleo Hospitality's primary goal when recruiting new employees is to fill vacancies with people who have the best available skills, abilities, or experience needed to perform the work. Decisions are made based on job-related criteria. When positions become available, qualified current employees are encouraged to apply. We also encourage current employees to recruit new talent.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Employment Classifications">
- **Exempt Employees** — Not subject to overtime pay provisions of the FLSA. Generally paid on a salary basis of at least $684/week in an administrative, professional, or management role.
- **Non-Exempt Employees** — Eligible for overtime pay.
- **Full-Time Employee** — Regularly scheduled to work at least 40 hours per week.
- **Part-Time Employee** — Regularly scheduled to work fewer than 40 hours per week.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Personnel Records">
It is important that Kaleo Hospitality always maintain accurate personnel records. You are responsible for notifying your immediate supervisor or Workstream Payroll of any change in name, home address, telephone number, marital status, number of dependents, immigration status, tax information, or any other pertinent information.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Orientation and Training">
We will provide an orientation and training session within the first few days after you begin work. In addition, Kaleo Hospitality may periodically offer additional training or educational programs. Some programs may be voluntary; others will be required.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Background Checks">
Kaleo Hospitality may conduct a background check on any applicant or employee with their signed consent. The background check may consist of prior employment verification, reference checks, education confirmation, criminal background, credit history, or other information as permitted by law. All offers of employment and continued employment are contingent upon a satisfactory background check.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Permits and Certifications">
- **Managers** must complete the **ServSafe Training Certificate within 30 days** of hire/promotion. Kaleo Hospitality will cover the course cost and one retake if they do not pass on the first try.
- A **Louisiana Food Handler's permit** may be required depending on your job description. If required, you must provide a copy to management within 30 days of hire.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Immigration Law">
Kaleo Hospitality complies with the Immigration Reform and Control Act of 1986 by employing only U.S. citizens and non-citizens authorized to work in the United States. All employees are asked on their first day to provide original documents verifying the right to work (Form I-9). If you cannot verify your right to work within three (3) days of hire, Kaleo Hospitality is required by law to terminate your employment.
</Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Attendance & Conduct

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="Attendance and Punctuality">
You are expected to report to work when scheduled. If you are delayed, you must call your immediate supervisor to state the reason. You must make every effort to speak directly with the manager.

If your absence is unexpected, you must attempt to reach your immediate supervisor no later than **one hour before you are due at work**. Leaving a voicemail or message with another staff member does not qualify as notifying your supervisor. Frequent absenteeism or tardiness may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Evaluation Period">
During the first **two months** of your employment, you will be in an "evaluation period." During this time, your supervisor will continually evaluate your performance and compatibility. Should your performance not meet Kaleo Hospitality's standards, your employment will be terminated. In some circumstances, the evaluation period may be extended.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Handling Guest Requests / Complaints">
In accordance with our philosophy that pleasing our guests is our number one priority, please try to honor every guest request. If you are uncertain whether we can honor a request, check with the manager. If a guest has a problem with any aspect of the food or service, contact your manager at once. It is your responsibility to ensure that no guest leaves the restaurant unhappy. **Do not allow a disgruntled guest to leave without the manager on duty being aware of the situation.**
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Fraternization Policy">
1. A "personal relationship" is defined as a relationship between individuals who have or have had a continuing relationship of a romantic or intimate nature.
2. Supervisors and managers are prohibited from dating subordinates and may be disciplined for such actions, up to and including termination.
3. Employees of Kaleo Hospitality are not permitted to date any managers/shift leaders within the system.
4. An employee who is involved in a personal relationship with another employee may not occupy a position in the same department as, work directly for, or supervise the employee with whom he or she is involved.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Employment of Relatives">
Kaleo Hospitality does not have a general prohibition against hiring relatives. However, an employee will generally not be hired, transferred, or promoted into a position where they will be managed, directly or indirectly, by a family member or romantic partner.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Children in the Workplace">
Children are not permitted at the workplace during hours of operation. Managers or Franchisees should be contacted in the event an emergency occurs.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Team Leader Requirements">
A Team Leader assumes some managerial tasks and must:
- Be at least 18 years of age
- Maintain a valid driver's license
- Be in good standing with the company
- Not be on a performance improvement plan
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Driving Safety">
Employees who drive on Kaleo Hospitality business must drive safely and responsibly. Key rules:

- A valid driver's license must be maintained as a condition of employment for driving positions.
- Cell phones are not permitted while driving except for navigation (set up before starting vehicle).
- Seat belts are required at all times.
- The use of alcohol, drugs, or impairing substances while driving is prohibited.
- All accidents must be reported promptly to law enforcement and the company.
- Kaleo Hospitality enforces hands-free driving at all times.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Inclement Weather">
Kaleo Hospitality is open for business unless there is a government-declared state of emergency or unless you are advised otherwise by your supervisor. There may be times when we will delay opening or close when severe weather develops. Use common sense and your best judgment when traveling to work in inclement weather.
</Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Dress Code & Appearance

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="Uniform Standards">
- Employees will wear a **company-issued shirt** from Kaleo Hospitality.
- **Pants**: Blue or black jeans, black slacks, or skirts. Not allowed: athletic/stretchy leggings, jeggings, jeans with holes/rips/embellishments, faded colors, or overly long pant legs.
- All clothing must fit properly. Skirts may not be form-fitting and no shorter than 2 inches above the knee.
- All clothing will be pressed, clean, and in good condition — no stains, fraying, holes, or discoloration.
- **Tucked in with a belt is required.**
- Solid white or black undershirts are allowed; sleeve should not extend past the t-shirt sleeve.
- Closed-toe shoes with heel fully covered. **Slip-resistant/rubber-soled shoes are mandatory.**
- **Hats**: Company-issued only, worn brim straight forward, clean and in good condition.

If clothing fails to meet these standards, the employee may be sent home to change and will receive a verbal warning for the first offense. Progressive discipline applies for continued violations.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Jewelry & Grooming">
**Jewelry:**
- Jewelry must be modest in size to prevent cross-contamination of food.
- All necklaces must be worn inside clothing.
- Rings limited to a wedding band (none on thumbs). In food prep areas, it must be covered with a foodservice glove.
- No wristwatches, bracelets, or wristbands permitted.

**Grooming:**
- Good personal hygiene required — clean-shaven, bathed, with appropriate deodorant.
- Hair must be clean, tied back if it falls around the face; no unnatural hair colors or eccentric styles.
- Hair and nails must follow local health department guidelines.
- Facial hair must be neatly trimmed to no longer than ¼" and not appear unkempt. Beard guards may be required.
- Fingernails must not extend past the fingertip. No fingernail gems or artificial nails (food prep hazard).
- Tattoos are allowed; offensive tattoos are not.
- Perfume or cologne must be subdued.
- Makeup must be subdued and worn in good taste.
</Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Technology & Workplace Policies

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="Cell Phones">
Cell phones are **not permitted while you are clocked in** unless you are on an assigned break in the approved break area and never in view of guests. Employees may use personal cell phones during work hours only for legitimate safety, security, or emergency purposes. The use of cameras on cell phones during work hours is prohibited to protect the privacy of Kaleo Hospitality and fellow employees.

Employees who fail to comply with cell phone policy will be written up. After the third write-up, the employee will be suspended. Continued violations may result in termination.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Social Media">
At no time should any live feeds or photos from within the restaurant be posted on social media without prior consent of the franchisees. Inappropriate postings — including discriminatory remarks, harassment, and threats of violence — will not be tolerated and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Internet & Email">
Access to the internet and email system is given principally for work-related activities. Incidental personal use is permitted but must not affect job performance. Kaleo Hospitality email, data storage, and network systems are company property and may be monitored at any time.

Never share your login or password with anyone. Never open links or attachments from unknown sources. Report any suspicious activity to your supervisor immediately.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Smoking">
Kaleo Hospitality prohibits smoking (including e-cigarettes) in the workplace and within a reasonable distance from any entrances, windows, or ventilation systems. Smoke breaks must be limited to official break time only. Smoking will not be permitted within view of the public. **Employees must thoroughly wash their hands after a smoke break.**
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Theft">
Kaleo Hospitality has **zero tolerance for theft**. If you are caught stealing, you will be terminated, and we will pursue legal action to the fullest possible extent of the law.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Firearms and Weapons">
Possession of firearms or knives over two inches on company property or company business is strictly prohibited.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Workplace Violence">
Kaleo Hospitality is committed to the safety and security of our employees. Acts of violence — including threats, aggressive behavior, or intentional physical contact without consent — will not be tolerated. All incidents will be thoroughly investigated and may involve law enforcement.
</Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Anti-Discrimination & Harassment

Kaleo Hospitality has a **zero tolerance policy** for harassment and discrimination. This applies to all terms and conditions of employment.

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="Discrimination Is Prohibited">
Kaleo Hospitality makes all employment decisions without regard to race, religion, color, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), national origin, disability, age, genetic information, or any other status protected under applicable federal, state, or local laws.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Workplace Harassment">
Harassment is defined as unwelcome conduct based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age (40 or older), disability, or genetic information. Harassment of any other person — including fellow employees, contractors, visitors, clients, or customers — whether at work or outside of work, is grounds for **immediate termination**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Sexual Harassment">
Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature. Such conduct may include inappropriate touching, lewd comments or jokes, suggestive material in the workplace, or repeated requests for dates.

Kaleo Hospitality encourages reporting of all perceived incidents of sexual harassment, regardless of who the offender may be.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How to Report Harassment or Discrimination">
There is no chain of command for reporting harassment or discrimination.

**STEP 1:** If possible, directly inform the harasser that the conduct is unwelcome and must stop.

**STEP 2:** IMMEDIATELY report the incident to the **Franchisee (Chad or Amanda Ducote)**. Please advise that your matter concerns a complaint under Kaleo Hospitality's anti-discrimination and anti-harassment policy so it can receive prompt attention.

**Anti-Retaliation:** An employee bringing a complaint in good faith will not be adversely affected as a result of making such a complaint. Retaliation is absolutely forbidden.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)">
Kaleo Hospitality prohibits discrimination of any kind against people with disabilities. Qualified applicants or employees who are disabled should request reasonable accommodation from their immediate supervisor, designated manager, or Human Resources. We will work with you to determine whether your disability can be reasonably accommodated.
</Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Pay & Benefits

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="Payroll">
- Pay periods are **bi-weekly**, beginning on Monday and ending on Sunday of the following week.
- Employees are paid **every other Friday**.
- When a payroll date falls on a holiday, employees will be paid on the last business day before the holiday.
- Pay is received via **direct deposit to a bank account** or **pay card** — your choice.
- Kaleo Hospitality will withhold taxes, insurance, and other authorized deductions as required by law.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Tips">
Tips are distributed per the tip pool policy outlined in your role agreement. If your employment is terminated, you will forfeit your participation in the tip pool and be paid only your base rate.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Vacation — GMs and Assistant Managers Only">
Vacation is available to **General Managers and Assistant Managers** who work an average of 38+ hours per week. Shift leaders and hourly team members are not eligible unless granted by Chad & Amanda.

**Vacation Eligibility:** Employees may not use vacation until after **1 year of full-time employment**.

**Accrual Schedule:**

| Years of Service | Days per Year | Hours per Bi-Weekly Pay Period |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| 1st year anniversary | 6 days | 1.846 hrs |
| 2nd–6th year | 12 days | 3.692 hrs |
| 7th–9th year | 15 days | 4.615 hrs |
| 10th year+ | 20 days | 6.154 hrs |

Vacation does not carry over at year end — unused time is forfeited. Vacation requests should be submitted **3 weeks in advance**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Sick Leave — GMs and Assistant Managers Only">
General Managers and Assistant Managers are allotted **3 days of sick leave per year** for legitimate medical reasons. Unused sick leave does not carry forward to the next year. Employees are not paid for unused sick leave upon termination or resignation.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Holidays">
Kaleo Hospitality observes **Thanksgiving** and **Christmas Day** as holidays. These holidays are recognized as **unpaid holidays**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Workers' Compensation">
You are covered by workers' compensation insurance. If you are injured or become ill on the job, you must **immediately report the injury or illness to your manager**. Failure to do so may delay your benefits or jeopardize your receipt of benefits.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Maternity Leave">
Kaleo Hospitality will provide up to **6 weeks of unpaid maternity leave** to the mother or father following the birth of a child or the placement of a child through adoption or foster care.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Nursing Mothers">
Employees may take reasonable, unpaid break time to express breast milk as needed for up to **one year** after the birth of a child. Kaleo Hospitality will provide a private place that is shielded from view and free from intrusion.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Military Service Leave">
Employees serving in the uniformed services may take unpaid military leave as needed. Servicemembers must provide advance written or verbal notice to Kaleo Hospitality, unless giving notice is impossible or precluded by military necessity.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Jury Duty">
Employees will be allowed leave to serve on a jury if summoned. Bring a copy of your summons as soon as you receive it. Kaleo Hospitality will compensate full-time employees for the difference between jury duty compensation and your daily pay for the **first 5 days** of jury service. If additional time is required, it will be granted without pay.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Travel Reimbursement">
The Company will reimburse reasonable, pre-approved business and travel expenses incurred during company business. All flights within the continental United States must be in coach. If you are unsure whether an expense will be covered, consult with your manager prior to traveling.
</Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Leaving Kaleo Hospitality

If you wish to resign, you are requested to notify your manager at least **2 weeks in advance** in writing.

- For **management and team leads**, a **30-day notice** is required for eligibility for rehire.
- If you fail to provide written 2-week notice, you will forfeit your participation in the tip pool, and **$250.00 will be deducted from your final paycheck**.
- All company equipment (shirts, name tags, etc.) must be returned. Unreturned equipment will be deducted from your last paycheck.
- Kaleo Hospitality asks all employees to participate in an **exit interview** with the franchisee prior to leaving.

## Safety

Kaleo Hospitality provides a clean, hazard-free, healthy, safe environment in which to work. As an employee, you have a duty to comply with safety rules and to take an active part in maintaining this environment:

- Observe all posted safety rules and adhere to all safety instructions from your supervisor.
- Keep your workspace neat, clean, and orderly.
- Report any accidents, injuries, unsafe equipment, or unsafe conditions to a supervisor immediately.
- If you become ill or get hurt at work, notify your manager immediately.

<Warning>
  Failure to report an injury immediately may result in a loss of benefits under state workers' compensation law.
</Warning>
