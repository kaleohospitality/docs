# Allergen Policy

Food allergies can be life-threatening. Every team member must follow this protocol without exception.

## The 9 Major Allergens (FDA)

1. Milk
2. Eggs
3. Fish
4. Shellfish
5. Tree nuts
6. Peanuts
7. Wheat
8. Soybeans
9. Sesame

## When a Guest Mentions an Allergy

<Steps>
  <Step title="Take it seriously">
    Never minimize or dismiss an allergy. Treat every request as potentially life-threatening.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inform the kitchen immediately">
    Notify the kitchen verbally AND mark the ticket clearly with the allergen (e.g., **ALLERGY: PEANUT**).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm what's safe">
    Check with the kitchen lead — do NOT guess. If unsure whether a dish is safe, say so honestly.
    > "I want to make sure I give you accurate information — let me double-check with our kitchen."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use dedicated equipment">
    The kitchen must use clean cookware, utensils, and surfaces for allergen orders. No shared equipment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deliver carefully">
    Bring the allergen dish out separately. Confirm with the guest before setting it down.
    > "This is your [dish], prepared allergen-free from [allergen]. Is there anything else I can confirm for you?"
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Cross-Contamination Prevention

- Change gloves before handling allergen-sensitive orders
- Use color-coded cutting boards if available
- Never place allergen-free items on surfaces that have touched the allergen
- Inform the guest if cross-contamination risk cannot be fully eliminated

## What to Say When You Can't Guarantee Safety

> "Our kitchen does prepare dishes containing [allergen], and while we take precautions, I can't guarantee zero cross-contact. I want to be upfront with you so you can make the best decision."

<Warning>
  If a guest experiences an allergic reaction on-premises, call 911 immediately, then notify the manager. Do not wait to see if symptoms resolve on their own.
</Warning>

## Menu Allergen Reference

Maintain an up-to-date allergen matrix in the host stand and at the manager station. Ask your manager for the current version or check the kitchen binder.
