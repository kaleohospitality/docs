# Menu Standards

Every dish that leaves the kitchen must meet these standards before it reaches the guest.

## Quality Check Before Every Plate Leaves

Ask yourself:

1. Is the portion correct per the recipe spec?
2. Does it look like the reference photo?
3. Is the temperature correct?
4. Is the plate clean with no smudges or drips?
5. Is the garnish fresh and properly placed?

If the answer to any of these is "no," the plate does not leave the kitchen.

## Portioning

All portions are set by recipe card. Do not eyeball — use the designated measuring tools.

| Category | Tool |
|----------|------|
| Proteins | Scale (grams/oz per recipe) |
| Sauces & dressings | Ladle or measured pour |
| Garnishes | Measured pinch or designated spoon |
| Beverages | Jigger for spirits; marked glassware for pours |

## Temperature Standards

All food temperatures are logged in **Jolt** using the Bluetooth thermometer or Zenput sensors. These are not optional — document every reading.

| Item | Safe Range | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Milk / custard mix | 34–40°F | Cold hold; check at AM and PM |
| Strawberries | 34–40°F | Cold hold; discard if out of range |
| Cheese slices | 34–40°F | Cold hold |
| Chili | 135°F+ | Hot hold; must maintain temp during service |
| Walk-in cooler | 34–40°F | Monitored by Zenput sensor |
| Walk-in freezer | 0°F or below | Monitored by Zenput sensor |

## Plating Presentation

- Follow the **reference photo** for every dish — photos are in the kitchen binder
- Keep the rim of the plate clean — wipe with a lint-free cloth before sending
- Garnishes go on **last**, just before service
- Hot plates go to the pass immediately — never hold a plated dish on the line

<Tip>
  If a new seasonal item is added to the menu, the kitchen lead will run a plating demo before the first service. Attend and ask questions.
</Tip>
